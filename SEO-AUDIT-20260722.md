# LANCHROM SEO and Search-Intent Audit

Date: 2026-07-22

## Research boundary

Google Trends reports normalized relative interest rather than absolute query counts. Exact average monthly searches require a signed-in Google Ads Keyword Planner account and vary by location, language and Search Network settings. This audit therefore does not invent numeric search volumes. It prioritizes recurring, commercially relevant query language found in current search results and maps it to pages where LANCHROM can answer the user's task directly.

## Layout and retention review

### Site-wide strengths retained

- Product, solution and application pages use one prominent H1 and concise metadata.
- Persistent side navigation supports scanning and internal discovery on large catalogs.
- Breadcrumbs clarify hierarchy on category and detail pages.
- Product pages expose samples, quotations and documentation without hiding the next step.
- Responsive title areas preserve readable text while showing the product or application image.

### Changes made in this revision

- Replaced the three Integrated Solutions overlays with the same brand turquoise and opacity used by the first homepage image; removed the horizontal mask block.
- Applied one complete pale-brand background to the global footer on every page.
- Added the six-step acetonitrile packaging process, explanatory text and container qualification notes.
- Published an original-data article with three Q1 scan figures, a comparison table, limitations, practical qualification steps, internal links and Article structured data.
- Added four searchable FAQ answers about LC-MS solvent scans, solvent blank comparison and lot qualification.
- Added method-first selection guidance to the Applications index and every application detail template.
- Added high-intent workflow paths to Solutions and detailed specification guidance to Mobile Phase Solutions.

## Measurement after deployment

1. Submit the updated sitemap in Google Search Console.
2. Request indexing for the new comparison article and updated packaging page.
3. Review Search Console queries by country after 28 and 90 days.
4. Export location-specific 12-month averages from Keyword Planner for the priority clusters above.
5. Track article-to-product clicks, sample requests and quote requests in GA4.
6. Expand only the queries that produce qualified engagement; avoid repeating keywords where the page does not answer the intent.

No ranking or traffic outcome is guaranteed. The implementation favors specific evidence, clear page purpose, useful internal links and readable technical detail over keyword repetition.
